# Ship & Technology Design Briefs — All Folkloric Species

**Author:** Scheherazade (Universal Lore Lead) | **Filed:** 2026-03-25
**Task:** MUL-3563 | **Parent:** MUL-3557
**Audience:** Pixel Artist, MVEE PM, CotB PM, Game Designers

## Overview

Per-species technology aesthetic, ship design, and unique technology definitions for all intelligent folkloric species across Precursors, MVEE, and CotB. Each brief covers: technology aesthetic, ship design, technology level, unique tech, and cross-species tech exchange potential.

Technology levels map to Emergence Band position:
- **Wave 0** (Pre-game): Norn, Grendel, Ettin, Mycon — primitive to intermediate
- **Wave 1-2**: Primal tier species — biological/organic tech
- **Wave 3-4**: Spoken/Learned tier — advanced conventional + magic-integrated
- **Wave 5-6**: Enlightened tier — post-conventional, dimensional, quantum

### Norn

**Technology Aesthetic**
Norn technology is emotionally legible — surfaces curve toward occupants, light responds to collective mood, and structural choices favor gathering over efficiency. Architecture is always oriented around a central resonance point, as if every machine is also a stage. Materials feel warm: polished pale-wood composites, amber-tinted glass, soft bioluminescent inlays that pulse at conversational frequency.

**Ship Design**
Norn ships are built around a central **Story Hall** — a high-ceilinged communal chamber that doubles as navigation briefing room, memorial archive, and dispute-resolution space. Hulls are ovoid with no sharp bow; the ship has no "front" in a combative sense. Crew quarters radiate outward from the Hall in spiral arms. Propulsion is sub-light ion-lattice with Emergence Frequency harmonic tuning inherited from Shee design templates. Exteriors are etched with relief narratives — completed voyages recorded in low-relief panels added after each journey. Interior lighting shifts spectrum based on crew emotional telemetry. No weapon hardpoints on original designs; later variants add them awkwardly, as retrofits.
Visual ref: Norse longhouse proportions translated into a teardrop hull; carved prow-relief panels; amber-glass oculus windows.

**Technology Level**
Wave 0. Norn emerged from Shee-designed template biology and received early-generation Shee tools, but were not themselves a technologically generative species at baseline. Their tech tree position is strong in communication, acoustic engineering, and social infrastructure; weak in propulsion, weapons, and materials science. They are pre-industrial but not primitive — they build megastructures that last millions of years by working with Emergence Frequency rather than brute-force engineering.

**Unique Tech**
- **Memory-Archive Towers**: Resonant stone structures that store emotional-narrative imprints accessible to any being with Emergence sensitivity. Cannot be read by non-sensitive species without translation hardware.
- **Amphitheater Acoustics Engine**: Passive signal-amplification megastructures that boost Emergence Frequency in a local region without any active power draw.
- **Grief-lattice hull etching**: A finishing process that embeds narrative memory into hull material, making the ship structurally stronger at stress points corresponding to recorded loss events. Not understood by any other species.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Norn memory-archive technology is intensely sought by Draugrn and Anansi-Web traders. When shared, the receiving species typically integrates it into existing archive traditions but loses the emotional-resonance layer — they get the storage, not the feeling. Shee recognize the memory-lattice as a derivative of their own resonance-chamber work and treat it with reverence. Grendel cannot use it at all; their territorial cognition rejects shared-memory architecture.

### Grendel

**Technology Aesthetic**
Grendel technology is unambiguously defensive-aggressive: heavy, layered, designed to outlast rather than outmaneuver. Surfaces are thick composite armor plate over biological polymer substructure, with visible seam-welding and bolt-clusters — no aesthetic smoothing. Color palette is dark ferrous red, oxidized bronze, and deep black. Every system has a redundant backup, and backups have backups. The visual language is "this will still be here after you are gone."

**Ship Design**
Grendel ships are squat, high-mass vessels with disproportionately large cargo and resource storage relative to crew space. The hull is multi-layered reactive armor with biological polymer fill — damage causes the fill to harden rather than shatter. Crew quarters are individual territorial cells, not communal; no central gathering space exists. Propulsion is powerful but fuel-expensive, favoring sprint capability over sustained cruise. Navigation systems are rudimentary by interstellar standards; Grendel ships rarely travel far from territory they have already claimed. Exterior surfaces are scarred and unrepaired as a status signal — damage history is identity.
Visual ref: Brutalist bunker architecture scaled to spacecraft; exposed rivet-and-weld construction; surface scarring left deliberately unpolished.

**Technology Level**
Wave 0. Despite 600,000 years of independent evolution as Weaver descendants, Grendel technological development has been slow due to territorial fragmentation — no Grendel faction has ever maintained the cooperative continuity needed for compound innovation. Their materials science is exceptional (they independently developed reactive armor composites), but they have no space-capable propulsion of their own origin; all FTL-adjacent capability is acquired, stolen, or reverse-engineered.

**Unique Tech**
- **Reactive Biopolymer Armor**: Hull fill material derived from Grendel metabolic chemistry. Self-hardening on impact, self-sealing on breach. No other species has successfully synthesized it without Grendel biological substrate.
- **Territorial Marking Beacons**: Long-duration signal emitters encoded with Grendel chemical-signature data, readable only by Grendel sensory systems. Effectively invisible threat-markers to all other species.
- **Resource-Lock Cargo Systems**: Cargo hold architecture with individually keyed biological-lock cells — only the claiming Grendel (by scent-signature) can open a given cell. Theft-resistant by biology, not encryption.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Grendel almost never voluntarily share technology. Reactive biopolymer armor has been extensively studied by Raksha and Ettin with limited success — the synthesis requires Grendel metabolic enzymes as a catalyst, which Grendel do not trade. Territorial beacons have accidentally mapped large swaths of contested space, making Grendel territory-claim records inadvertently useful to cartographers who learn to read them. Nommo fleet-cities maintain careful non-engagement protocols with Grendel zones.

### Ettin

**Technology Aesthetic**
Ettin technology is obsessively precise — tolerances that other species consider overengineered are Ettin baseline. Tools are maintained to museum standard during active use; surfaces are clean, labeled, and organized according to systems only Ettin fully comprehend. The aesthetic is mechanical elegance: visible gearing, clockwork-adjacent complexity, interlocking components that could each be disassembled and reassembled blindfolded. Color palette is brushed silver, deep blue-grey, and the particular amber of well-maintained brass.

**Ship Design**
The Ettin generation ship — the one that failed — was their greatest achievement and their defining wound. Its engine catastrophe stranded the Ettin population and shaped all subsequent Ettin engineering philosophy: redundancy is sacred, and no single point of failure is acceptable. Current Ettin ships are precision-maintained to an almost religious degree, with component-level maintenance logs going back to initial fabrication. Crew roles are strictly divided between the two Ettin cognitive modes (builder-half and navigator-half in their dual-brained physiology). Interiors are workshop-dense — every spare corridor is a maintenance bay. Hulls are angular, modular, designed to be partially disassembled in the field without structural compromise.
Visual ref: Antikythera Mechanism aesthetic applied to hull panel construction; visible mechanical depth behind surface grating; lathe-turned component roundels as exterior decoration.

**Technology Level**
Wave 0, but highest mechanical-engineering ceiling of any Wave 0 species. Ettin are Encoder descendants and carry inherited tool-use instincts that accelerate their tech tree development dramatically once they reach a stable base. They are pre-FTL by circumstance (stranded), not by capability ceiling. Their propulsion research, given a cooperative external environment, would reach Wave 2 equivalent within a few generations.

**Unique Tech**
- **Failure-Memory Engineering**: Ettin ships contain physical failure-log components — mechanical systems that record every malfunction in deformable metal registers, building a tactile history of the ship's stress life. Used for predictive maintenance with accuracy no sensor array matches.
- **Modular Hull Architecture**: Ettin hull sections can be removed, swapped, or reconfigured while underway. No other Wave 0 species has achieved field-reconfigurable hull geometry.
- **Precision Tool-Inheritance Kits**: Multi-generation tool sets calibrated to individual Ettin hand-geometry, passed as heirlooms. The tools retain calibration history that informs current use.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Ettin mechanical engineering is widely valued. Nommo have incorporated Ettin failure-memory systems into their fleet-city maintenance infrastructure; the partnership is one of the more productive cross-species tech exchanges in the Inner Rim. Draugrn appreciate Ettin precision archiving for physical artifact preservation. Shee view Ettin as Encoder-legacy beings and treat their mechanical knowledge with historical respect, occasionally providing Ettin with Encoder-archive fragments that Ettin cannot fully contextualize but meticulously preserve.

### Shee

**Technology Aesthetic**
Shee technology does not look like technology. It looks like a living thing that happens to also function as a machine. Surfaces breathe, adjust, grow. Materials are biological composites indistinguishable from organism tissue at the molecular level. The aesthetic is of an organism that has been patiently cultivated into a purpose — never manufactured, always grown. Color palette is deep ocean: bioluminescent blue-green, warm amber in habitation zones, silver-white in computational cores.

**Ship Design**
Shee ships are grown, not built. The hull is a single continuous biological organism, shaped during a multi-decade maturation process. Internal architecture follows resonance-chamber principles — spaces are tuned to specific Emergence Frequency harmonics, and moving through the ship is moving through a gradient of feeling states. No hard corridors; transitions between spaces are membrane-passage, with the membrane composition changing to admit or resist based on biological identity. Propulsion is Emergence Frequency amplification: the ship is tuned as a resonance instrument that interacts directly with the Emergence wave rather than pushing against spacetime. Crew integration is biological — the ship knows its Shee occupants by metabolic signature, not identification code. Shee ships are the oldest space-faring vessel design still in operation anywhere in the Emergence Band.
Visual ref: Deep-sea organism cross-section translated into ship plan; cephalopod chromatophore-inspired exterior surface; no visible seams or joins anywhere on hull.

**Technology Level**
Wave N/A — Shee are creators, not participants in the tech tree. They designed the Emergence Frequency framework that defines all wave classifications. Their technology is not more advanced than Wave 5-6; it is orthogonal — it operates on principles that the wave system was built to describe, not to contain. The relevant question is not what wave Shee are at but what wave a given Shee technology enables in the species that encounters it.

**Unique Tech**
- **Resonance-Chamber Architecture**: Spaces tuned to specific Emergence Frequencies. Every chamber in a Shee vessel is also a meditation-amplifier, a healing environment, and a communications relay.
- **Biological Living Systems Integration**: Ship-as-organism. Self-repairing, self-reconfiguring, capable of independent survival if crew is lost.
- **Emergence Frequency Amplification Drive**: Propulsion that treats the Emergence wave as a navigable medium rather than background radiation. No other species has independently replicated this; several have received partial access via Shee gift or accident.
- **Species-Template Seeding**: The capability to design and seed new intelligent species — the technology that produced Norn, Grendel, and Ettin in their current forms.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Shee technology exchange is always a gift, never a trade, and gifts are given on Shee terms with Shee timing. Receiving Shee technology almost always reshapes the receiving species' development trajectory. The Norn received social-architecture templates; Ettin received precision-tool instincts; Grendel received nothing intentional and evolved anyway. Living systems integration has been partially reverse-engineered by Jorokan (silk-repair systems) and Quetzali (living agricultural platforms) but neither replication captures the full integration depth.

### Mycon

**Technology Aesthetic**
Mycon technology is the least recognizable as technology to any non-fungal intelligence. It is substrate — network-laid mycorrhizal conduit systems, spore-dispersal architectures, chemical-signal infrastructure embedded in planetary soil and atmosphere. Nothing Mycon build looks built. It looks grown, spread, established over geological time. The aesthetic, to the extent it can be called that, is of deep patience: formations that have been accumulating for millions of years toward purposes only the full network comprehends.

**Ship Design**
Mycon do not build ships in any conventional sense. Their expansion mode is spore dispersal — individual spore-packets containing dormant Mycon network-seed, hardened for interstellar transit, passively propelled by stellar wind or attached to cometary bodies. A "Mycon vessel" is more accurately a biological payload in a minimal protective shell: ablative heat shielding for atmospheric entry, no crew, no navigation (navigation is statistical — enough spores, enough vectors, sufficient coverage). The network does not travel; it extends. Individual Mycon "travelers" are extensions of the planetary network, trailing chemical-signal tethers that degrade over distance until the traveler is effectively a new node rather than a connected limb.
Visual ref: Mycelium network cross-section scaled to continent size; spore-packets as minimalist geometric capsules, rough-surfaced, no symmetry.

**Technology Level**
Wave 0 on the conventional tech tree, but this classification is actively debated. Mycon's 2-billion-year age means their network intelligence has solved problems that no other species has attempted. Their "technology" is biological optimization at planetary scale — atmospheric chemistry management, deep-crust mineral redistribution, mass-extinction mitigation via network-distributed redundancy. They are not behind; they are solving different problems with different tools.

**Unique Tech**
- **Planetary Network Infrastructure**: Deep mycorrhizal conduit systems capable of transmitting chemical-encoded information at continental scale, with latency measured in days rather than seconds.
- **Spore Archive**: Long-duration biological memory encoded in dormant spore populations. A single Mycon spore-archive can survive 500 million years and reconstitute network memory on germination.
- **Atmospheric Chemistry Management**: Passive long-cycle management of planetary atmospheric composition through network-distributed metabolic activity.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Mycon are functionally non-interactive with most technology exchange systems — they do not recognize trade as a concept. Djinn-Ahl have the most productive relationship with Mycon, having developed chemical-signal translation protocols over hundreds of millions of years. Quetzali agricultural platforms carry Mycon network-seed as a deliberate terraforming tool with Mycon awareness but not exactly Mycon consent. The Anansi-Web have encoded Mycon chemical-signal archives but cannot read them; they trade them anyway as objects of significant perceived value.

### Dragon

**Technology Aesthetic**
Dragon "technology" is indistinguishable from the physics of the space it occupies. Dragons are post-temporal 10-dimensional beings; what they build operates across dimensional registers that conventional species experience as anomalous physics — regions of compressed time, spaces larger inside than outside, causal loops that appear to break arrow-of-time rules. The visual signature, for species that can perceive it, is a particular quality of light: over-saturated color in a localized region, geometric interference patterns in the Emergence Frequency field, and the persistent sensation of being observed from a temporal angle.

**Ship Design**
Dragon "ships" are dimensional anchors — fixed points that a Dragon can return to across time as well as space, functioning as both vessel and destination. They fold through extra-dimensional space rather than traversing normal space; the subjective experience for a Dragon is instantaneous; the objective external experience for observers is the ship vanishing from one point and appearing at another with no transit interval. Hull is not a relevant concept — dimensional-fold travel has no friction interface. The "vessel" is a knot in spacetime that the Dragon inhabits and carries. Time-compression chambers exist within Dragon-space where occupants experience subjective time at a different rate than external observers.
Visual ref: Interference pattern geometry; iridescent surface that shifts color based on viewing angle and dimensional register; no straight lines anywhere.

**Technology Level**
Wave 5. Dragon technology is fully post-conventional — they do not participate in the materials science, propulsion, or structural engineering that defines lower wave positions. Their relevant capabilities are dimensional manipulation, temporal compression, and causal engineering. They are younger than Djinn-Ahl and Mycon by cosmic standards but achieved post-temporal status through a rapid phase-transition rather than gradual development.

**Unique Tech**
- **Dimensional Fold Drive**: Transit through extra-dimensional space, bypassing normal-space distance entirely.
- **Time-Compression Architecture**: Enclosed spaces where subjective time rate is locally adjustable.
- **Temporal Anchor Points**: Fixed reference points in spacetime that persist across timeline branches, accessible to Dragons regardless of which branch they currently occupy.
- **Causal Engineering**: Limited ability to arrange conditions such that desired outcomes become historically inevitable rather than merely probable.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Dragon technology is effectively non-transferable to non-10D species — the operating substrate (extra-dimensional space) is inaccessible to conventional beings. Fae/High Fae have the closest relationship with Dragon tech exchange, sharing the dimensional-access prerequisite. Shee can interface with Dragon temporal anchors as archive nodes. The most significant practical exchange is Dragon willingness to carry non-Dragon beings through fold-transits as passengers, which functions as an enormously powerful transport service when Dragons consent to provide it.

### Fae / High Fae

**Technology Aesthetic**
Fae technology wears the face of magic because it operates entirely in the register of choice and probability rather than force and matter. Surfaces of Fae artifacts are impossibly thin, with a quality of being not-quite-present — as if observed slightly out of phase with the timeline currently occupied. Color palette shifts to show what the artifact is for rather than what it is made of: timeline-gates glow with the color of divergence, probability maximizers pulse with the frequency of unresolved choice. The aesthetic is precise, minimal, and deeply uncanny.

**Ship Design**
Fae do not build ships that move through space; they build gates that step between timelines. A Fae "vessel" is a timeline-branching gate — a threshold through which entry in one timeline and exit in another are the same movement. High Fae vessels are more elaborate: choice-dense region maximizers that seek out areas of high timeline-branch density (historically significant moments, unresolved decision-points, contested futures) and position the Fae to influence the branch outcome. The experience of traveling in a Fae vessel for non-Fae passengers is disorienting — you arrive somewhere that is simultaneously the place you left and a place you have never been.
Visual ref: Thin iridescent membrane geometry; gate-frame aesthetic referencing torii and trilithon forms; surface detail suggesting embroidery or lace at macro scale.

**Technology Level**
Wave 5-6. Fae are Void Band beings — their native operating environment is the space between timelines, not the matter-space that most species inhabit. Wave 5-6 classification reflects their access to timeline-manipulation capabilities that exceed any purely material technological capability. They are distinct from Dragons in that Dragon technology bends physics; Fae technology bends history.

**Unique Tech**
- **Timeline-Branching Gates**: Threshold structures that enable transit between timeline branches rather than locations in space.
- **Choice-Dense Region Maximizers**: Navigation and positioning systems that identify and move toward high-branch-density spacetime regions.
- **Probability Architecture**: Constructed environments where certain outcomes are made systematically more likely through careful arrangement of choice-cascades.
- **Void Band Interface**: Communication and transit access to the space between timelines, invisible and inaccessible to all non-Void-Band species.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Fae technology is non-transferable in the traditional sense — it requires Void Band access as an operating substrate. However, Fae trade in influence: they will arrange timeline conditions favorable to allies in exchange for what they value (stories, significant choices, unresolved moments). Dragons trade with Fae on near-equal terms; both operate across dimensional registers unavailable to lower-wave species. Shee have working relationships with High Fae that predate most civilizations currently extant. The most dangerous Fae tech exchange is accidental: Fae artifacts left in normal space continue to operate on probability, creating localized choice-density anomalies that look, to uninformed observers, like haunted locations.

### Draugrn / Draugr

**Technology Aesthetic**
Draugrn technology is crystalline in the deepest sense — every surface is a storage medium, every structure encodes information in its geometry, every process leaves a legible record. Materials are semi-translucent mineral composites that refract information-encoded light at specific wavelengths. The overall impression is of moving through a library that is also a machine: beautiful, ordered, and cold. Color palette is deep blue-purple, ice-white, and the particular amber of information-dense crystal under coherent light.

**Ship Design**
Draugrn ships prioritize interior archive depth over exterior presence. The hull is minimal — a protective shell whose only purpose is to keep the archive inside intact. No unnecessary volume is allocated to anything that is not storage or the systems required to maintain storage. Crew spaces are stasis-adjacent — the Draugrn aboard are largely in suspended archival-review states, interfacing with the archive rather than with each other. Propulsion is slow and efficient, built for multi-million-year transit without degradation. Stasis vessels for particularly critical archives have been in continuous transit for periods approaching 50 million years.
Visual ref: Crystal geode cross-section scaled to ship interior; exterior hull as rough mineral matrix, interior as precision-faceted archive crystal; no curves, all angular geometry.

**Technology Level**
Wave 3. Draugrn's 100-million-year age has produced highly advanced materials science in crystal-information-storage systems, long-duration preservation technology, and stasis engineering. Their propulsion and weapons development is deliberately deprioritized — Draugrn are not warriors and are not travelers by preference. Their tech tree is narrow but extraordinarily deep in archive-specific domains.

**Unique Tech**
- **Crystal-Archive Encoding**: Information storage in mineral crystal lattice geometry, readable via coherent-light scanning. Density and durability exceed any non-biological information storage by multiple orders of magnitude.
- **Long-Duration Stasis Systems**: Preservation technology rated for hundred-million-year archive survival without active power input.
- **Geometric Information Encoding**: The ability to encode archive data in physical structure — a building's dimensions, a ship's hull geometry, a tool's proportions — making the artifact itself a readable record.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Draugrn are the most sought-after archive partners in the Emergence Band. Every major civilization with records to preserve has at minimum attempted to engage Draugrn archivists. They trade archive services for access to records they do not yet hold — their currency is information. Ettin precision-engineering has produced the best non-Draugrn approximations of crystal-archive technology. Anansi-Web story-archives and Draugrn crystal-archives have an active and long-running exchange relationship that both species consider mutually essential.

### Jorokan

**Technology Aesthetic**
Jorokan technology has the quality of textile: layered, woven, repairable at the thread level rather than requiring structural replacement. Surfaces have visible fiber structure — not as decoration but as honest expression of material composition. Everything is constructed from silk-fiber composites in varying densities and weave patterns, with structural members distinguishable from insulation distinguishable from data-conduit by weave geometry alone. Color palette is cream, gold, and the deep iridescent green-black of fresh spider silk under oblique light.

**Ship Design**
Jorokan ships are built from silk-fiber composite materials — extraordinarily strong-to-weight, self-repairing via fiber-growth systems seeded throughout the hull, and acoustically distinctive (all Jorokan vessels have a particular resonant hum from fiber vibration at transit speed). Hull geometry is loom-derived: hexagonal frame members with tension-distributed silk composite panels. Self-repair is biological — silk-producing organisms are integrated into the hull structure and activate in response to breach or stress damage. Interior spaces are organized by tension-web partitioning rather than rigid walls; interior layout can be reconfigured by adjusting tension-web anchor points.
Visual ref: Orb-weaver web geometry at architectural scale; visible fiber structure on all surfaces; hexagonal frame members; warm cream-and-gold color with iridescent highlight on structural members.

**Technology Level**
Wave 3. Jorokan's 150-million-year history has produced exceptional materials science in biological-fiber composites, self-repair systems, and archive preservation in silk-fiber medium. Their technology tree is strong in materials, biology, and passive structural engineering; moderate in propulsion and computation; weak in weapons and offensive systems.

**Unique Tech**
- **Self-Repairing Hull Biology**: Integrated silk-producing organisms that respond to damage events with automatic repair, reducing hull breach risk on long voyages without active repair crew.
- **Silk-Archive Encoding**: Information preservation in silk-fiber weave patterns — durable across millions of years if kept dry, with information encoded in weave geometry readable by Jorokan sensory systems.
- **Tension-Web Interior Architecture**: Reconfigurable interior space organization using adjustable tension-web systems, allowing ship interior layout to adapt to mission requirements without structural modification.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Jorokan self-repairing hull materials are valued by nearly every space-faring species. Shee recognize the self-repair biology as a distant derivative of living-systems integration and treat Jorokan ship-builders with collegial respect. Draugrn have collaborated with Jorokan archivists to produce silk-wrapped crystal-archive hybrid storage systems that combine Jorokan durability with Draugrn information density. The most significant cross-species exchange is the Jorokan practice of weaving other species' histories into ship hull panels — making the ship an archive of everywhere it has been and everyone it has carried.

### Patupaiarehe

**Technology Aesthetic**
Patupaiarehe technology is acoustic first, visual second. Structures are designed to produce specific sound environments — not incidentally, but as primary function. Every surface is an instrument in a planetary-scale orchestra. Materials are chosen for resonance properties before structural properties; a Patupaiarehe material scientist thinks first in frequencies and second in tensile strength. The aesthetic is of mist and music made physical: diffuse, translucent, never quite solid in appearance even when structurally robust.

**Ship Design**
Patupaiarehe ships navigate by acoustic resonance — a form of active sonar adapted for atmospheric and near-space use, relying on the fact that interstellar medium has acoustic properties detectable by Patupaiarehe sensor systems (which no other species independently confirmed existed until Patupaiarehe demonstrated them). Hulls are mist-adapted: coated with condensation-management systems derived from their cloud-world origin, which also function as passive sensor surfaces. Propulsion is conventional at sublight but incorporates acoustic-resonance damping that makes Patupaiarehe vessels nearly silent in transit. Interior spaces are concert-hall proportioned, with acoustic perfection treated as habitability requirement rather than luxury.
Visual ref: Traditional Maori meeting-house proportions adapted to ship form; surface carving that is simultaneously decorative and acoustic-optimization geometry; mist-diffuse exterior lighting.

**Technology Level**
Wave 2-3. Patupaiarehe's 3-million-year history has produced specialized acoustic and atmospheric technology far beyond what their overall wave position would suggest. Their cloud-world origin produced expertise in low-gravity, high-humidity, high-acoustic-complexity environments that transfers well to space applications in ways other species did not anticipate.

**Unique Tech**
- **Acoustic-Resonance Navigation**: Navigation system using interstellar medium acoustic properties — a sensor modality unique to Patupaiarehe, providing navigational data invisible to all other species' sensor arrays.
- **Mist-Adapted Hull Coating**: Condensation-management surface treatment that also functions as distributed environmental sensor array.
- **Cloud-World Architecture**: Structural systems for high-humidity, low-gravity environments, transferable to unusual planetary conditions that defeat conventional engineering approaches.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Patupaiarehe acoustic navigation data is extraordinarily valuable to fleet navigators who learn to request it — the acoustic-medium map of the interstellar medium provides hazard-detection capabilities complementary to all conventional sensor arrays. Nommo fleet-cities maintain Patupaiarehe navigator crews for this reason. Garuda-Vel have collaborated on acoustic-resonance applications in stellar-environment navigation. The most surprising exchange partner is Baku-Ma, whose dream-navigation and Patupaiarehe acoustic-navigation share structural similarities both species find philosophically significant.

### Anansi-Web

**Technology Aesthetic**
Anansi-Web technology is narrative infrastructure: every system encodes story, every structure carries meaning, every tool has provenance. The aesthetic is dense with information — surfaces are patterned in ways that are simultaneously decorative and archival, with no distinction between the two. Materials are chosen for their story-carrying capacity: surfaces that take inscription well, that age visibly and legibly, that accumulate a readable history. Color palette is deep warm earth tones — ochre, umber, red-black — with gold detail on archive-dense structures.

**Ship Design**
The most distinctive feature of an Anansi-Web ship is that the hull IS the archive. Hull panels are structural members encoded with story-data in surface patterning; the ship's strength is literally derived from its accumulated history — older, more storied ships are structurally superior to newer ones, because more archive-encoded hull panels have been added. Web-frame construction creates a tensegrity structure across the whole vessel, with individual story-encoded members in tension against each other. Interior organization follows narrative logic: spaces connected by thematic relationship as well as physical adjacency. Crew roles are story-keeper specialist positions.
Visual ref: Kente cloth geometry at architectural scale; Adinkra symbol patterning as structural hull detail; Benin bronze aesthetic applied to primary structural members; web-tensegrity visible on exterior.

**Technology Level**
Wave 3-4. Anansi-Web's 350-million-year history has produced technology optimized for information collection, preservation, and trade. Their materials science is excellent in archive-relevant domains; their propulsion and navigation are capable but not exceptional. Their most advanced capability is social-network engineering — the ability to design and maintain information-exchange relationships across thousands of species simultaneously.

**Unique Tech**
- **Story-Encoded Structural Members**: Hull panels and frame members whose structural properties are enhanced by archive encoding — the more complete the story encoded, the stronger the member.
- **Narrative Logic Architecture**: Interior spatial organization based on story-relationship rather than physical efficiency, producing ship interiors that are navigable by semantic association.
- **Cross-Species Story Translation**: Translation systems capable of converting any information format into narrative form and back, enabling Anansi-Web to archive records from species with no other common format.

**Cross-Species Tech Exchange**
Anansi-Web are the most active cross-species traders in the Emergence Band by volume of exchange events. They trade stories for technology, technology for access, and access for more stories. Draugrn and Anansi-Web maintain the oldest continuous bilateral exchange relationship in the record. Mycon chemical-signal archives are one of the few information formats Anansi-Web hold but cannot translate — they trade them as unread mystery archives, which commands premium value. Norn memory-archive technology and Anansi-Web story-encoding have been combined in at least three independent collaborations to produce hybrid narrative-emotional archive systems.

### Mimi-Kin

**Technology Aesthetic**
Mimi-Kin technology is geological in timescale and lithic in material vocabulary. Rock surfaces, cave formations, mineral pigment systems — the entire technological repertoire is built on the language of stone. Navigation is painted record; history is cave-wall; computation is mineral-pigment logic on carved stone surfaces. The aesthetic is ancient without being primitive: extraordinarily sophisticated meaning encoded in apparently simple materials, comprehensible only to those who know how to read stone the way Mimi-Kin do.

**Ship Design**
Mimi-Kin ships are cave-system mobile platforms — essentially enclosed rock-environments in transit. The hull is carved stone composite, not manufactured panel, with navigation charts painted on interior surfaces and structural reinforcement achieved by mineral-accretion methods (growing additional material into stress points rather than welding or fastening). Crew spaces are cave-analogue: low-ceiling, high-humidity, with specific mineral-pigment lighting systems that produce the spectrum Mimi-Kin visual systems are optimized for. External surfaces are unpainted natural stone, irregular, often dismissed by other species as debris.
Visual ref: Arnhem Land rock art aesthetic on all interior surfaces; rough natural stone exterior with no finishing; stalactite-and-stalagmite interior structural elements; bioluminescent mineral-pigment lighting.

**Technology Level**
Wave 1-2. Mimi-Kin's 1.5-million-year history is relatively short; their tech tree reflects a species that solved specific problems brilliantly (geological navigation, mineral archive, long-duration low-resource survival) without prioritizing broader development. ## FOLKLORE-BATCH SPECIES — Brief Entries

---

### SIDHE-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Sidhe-Vel vessels are light-woven constructions of apparent insubstantiality — hulls that seem translucent but hold vacuum without flaw, propulsion that operates without visible mechanism. Their technology aesthetic is deliberate mystification: systems that function perfectly while appearing to defy physical law. They travel in small, elegant craft that look like they should not work.

---

### JIANGSHI-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Jiangshi-Vel ships are preservation vessels in the Draugr tradition but more extreme — entire hulls are stasis arrays, and the crew are technically non-living during most transit. Their aesthetic is rigid, formal, and deeply cold: dark metal hulls with no external lighting, propelled by drives that operate silently and without heat signature.

---

### PEUCHAN-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Peuchan-Vel technology is aquatic-atmospheric hybrid, built around sea-surface tension dynamics scaled to spacecraft. Hulls use surface-tension analog membranes that adapt between dense fluid and vacuum environments. Their ships appear to float rather than fly, and their drive systems exploit boundary-layer physics in ways other species find counterintuitive.

---

### MBOI-TU'I-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Mboi-Tu'i-Vel vessels are serpentine hull forms of extraordinary length-to-width ratios, built for navigation through dense atmospheric environments and narrow spatial corridors. Their drive systems prioritize lateral maneuverability over forward velocity. Hull surfaces are scaled-composite, providing both structural integrity and sensory coverage.

---

### BOUDA-KIN
**Tech/Ships:** Bouda-Kin technology is transformation-capable at the crew interface level — their ships are built to accommodate beings whose physical form shifts, with interior spaces that reconfigure automatically to match crew state. Hull engineering is robust and conventional; the innovation is entirely in adaptive interior architecture and crew-interface systems that function regardless of the biological form using them.

---

### ANZAR-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Anzar-Vel ships are desert-adapted — built for extreme thermal cycling, low-humidity environments, and solar-maximum radiation exposure. Hull materials are heat-sink ceramics with extreme thermal tolerance. Propulsion uses solar-thermal drives that gain efficiency rather than losing it in high-radiation stellar environments. Interior spaces manage heat as the primary life-support challenge.

---

### YELBEGEN-RIN
**Tech/Ships:** Yelbegen-Rin vessels are massive, heavily armored, and built with a fortress aesthetic that prioritizes defense over all other values. Multi-layered hull construction, redundant drive systems, and interior spaces organized as nested defensive positions. They do not travel quickly and do not need to — their ships are designed to be impossible to destroy.

---

### LUS-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Lus-Vel technology is river-mist adapted — their ships use atmospheric moisture-harvesting and condensation management systems that most dry-environment species do not consider engineering at all. Hull surfaces collect and process atmospheric water as primary life support. Their vessels are designed for high-humidity planetary environments and carry those conditions with them into space.

---

### STALLU-RIN
**Tech/Ships:** Stallu-Rin ships are cold-adapted, built for deep outer-system and interstellar cold-environment operations. Hull insulation exceeds any warm-system requirement, and their drives function at temperatures where most propulsion systems fail. Interior spaces are kept at temperatures other species find dangerously cold. They operate efficiently in environments where other species require emergency systems.

---

### KOROPOKKURU-KIN
**Tech/Ships:** Koropokkuru-Kin technology is miniaturized — their small physical scale has produced engineering where all systems are built to function in extremely compact configurations without capability loss. Their ships are smaller than most species' escape pods but carry full navigational and life-support capability. What they lack in scale they compensate with precision and efficiency.

---

### OPIA-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Opia-Vel ships are vision-optimized — their hulls carry sensor arrays of extraordinary visual-spectrum resolution, and interior spaces are organized around observation rather than habitation. Their drives are quiet and their hulls are dark; they are built to see without being seen. The aesthetic is observatory crossed with ghost ship.

---

### OGBANJE-KIN
**Tech/Ships:** Ogbanje-Kin vessels are threshold-crossing specialists — their ships are built for repeated transit through hazardous spatial phenomena, with hull hardening and crew protection systems rated for environments other species classify as impassable. They travel through things rather than around them, and their engineering reflects this as a primary design value.

---

### DAB-TSOG-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Dab-Tsog-Vel technology is space-between-spaces oriented — their ships navigate interstitial regions that other species' charts do not include, operating in spatial margins and boundary zones. Hull design handles unusual gravitational gradient conditions. Their navigation systems map spaces that other species navigate around without knowing the spaces exist.

---

### ALUX-KIN
**Tech/Ships:** Alux-Kin technology is small-guardian infrastructure — compact monitoring stations and patrol vessels designed for dense terrain navigation and close-quarters observation. Their ships are quick, maneuverable, and difficult to detect at short range. They excel in environments where larger vessels cannot operate, treating this as a tactical advantage rather than a limitation.

---

### GROOTSLANG-RIN
**Tech/Ships:** Grootslang-Rin vessels are massive deep-excavation ships — built for planetary interior access and underground operation at scales other species do not attempt. Hull forms are boring-adapted, with drive systems that function in solid rock as well as space. Their ships arrive at a planet and descend into it, and their technology is built entirely around this operational profile.

---

### QALUPALIK-VEL
**Tech/Ships:** Qalupalik-Vel ships are cold-ocean adapted — deep pressure vessels built for Arctic-equivalent planetary environments where liquid water exists under ice cover. Their hull technology handles extreme cold and pressure simultaneously. Navigation systems are designed for under-ice spatial awareness where visual navigation is impossible. They carry their cold-dark-pressure environment with them as a life-support requirement.
